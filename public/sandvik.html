Sandvik Internship Experience

Overview
Mechanical Engineering Intern | 16th August 2021 – 17th September 2021

As a Mechanical Engineering Intern at Sandvik Asia Pvt. Ltd., I worked under the mentorship of Ms. Anagha Patil in an online mode for 33 days. My project, titled Introduction to Inserts Application, Manufacturing Processes & Design, focused on understanding metal cutting technology and applying it through a CAD-based project using AutoCAD.

About Sandvik

Founded in 1862, Sandvik is a Swedish multinational engineering company renowned for its innovation in metal cutting, digital manufacturing, and tooling solutions. With approximately 37,000 employees globally, Sandvik specializes in:

  • Tools and tooling systems
  • Mining and rock excavation equipment
  • Advanced stainless steel and alloy products

The Sandvik Coromant division is celebrated for technologies like CoroPlus, Inveio Coating, and PrimeTurning™.

Internship Objectives
  • Understand the application and manufacturing process of inserts
  • Gain theoretical and practical knowledge in metal cutting technology
  • Learn about tool design, insert geometry, and material selection
  • Apply concepts in a CAD-based project using AutoCAD
What I Learned
Core Concepts
  • Machinability and ISO material classification (P, M, K, N, S, H)
  • Insert coatings (CVD, PVD) and their impact on wear resistance
  • Cutting edge design, chip formation, and wear patterns
  • Types of cutting operations: Turning, Parting and Grooving, Threading, Milling, Drilling & Boring
  • Tool Holding Systems: Coromant Capto, ER collets, hydraulic chucks
  • Machining Economics: Productivity = Output / Input
  • Insert Manufacturing Lifecycle: Pressing → Sintering → Grinding → Coating → Inspection → Labelling → Delivery
Project Work

I completed a technical drawing project in AutoCAD under Ms. Anagha Patil’s guidance. The task involved converting orthographic views of two inserts into isometric projections, emphasizing precision and CAD accuracy. Through feedback sessions, I corrected mistakes and gained hands-on experience with industry-standard drawings.

Tools & Technologies Used
  • AutoCAD for 2D and isometric drawing
  • Sandvik’s proprietary learning resources
  • Online modules and reference standards (ISO 26623, ISO 13399)
Challenges Faced

The online mode limited communication and access to physical machinery. However, strong mentorship and comprehensive resources from Sandvik helped me overcome these challenges effectively.

Certification

I received a certificate from Sandvik Coromant validating my completion of the 33-day training, recognizing my dedication, sincerity, and excellent performance.

Key Takeaways
  • Deep understanding of metal cutting technology
  • Insight into the end-to-end insert production process
  • Application of theoretical knowledge into technical drawings
  • Enhanced confidence in core mechanical design and tooling concepts
